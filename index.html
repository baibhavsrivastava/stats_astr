Statistics for Physics and Astronomy

A Short Summary

Baibhav Srivastava
Some general comments to keep in mind (these might sound repetative, but one can't stress them enough!):
  1. Get to know your data before you start doing any statistical analysis. This will help decide what techniquues to use and if they are appropriate.
  2. A lot depends on the actual question you are trying to answer. The same data set can be analyzed in multiple ways to answer different questions.
  3. Do not randomly throw a test on your data. Any statistical test will give you some or the other result. It is on the user to decide what test to use and what result to interpret.
  4. Do not blindly trust the results of a statistical test. Always check the assumptions of the test and the conditions under which it is valid.
  5. The computer does not have any scientific understanding. It will do what you tell it to do. Use your domain knowledge to decide on proper preprocessing, tests to use, and interpretation of results.
  6. A lot of these tests depend on some parameters like the significance level, the number of bins, the number of clusters, etc. Always check the sensitivity of your results to these parameters. You, as the human (and not the computer), have to decide on the best values for these parameters, for your specific problem.
